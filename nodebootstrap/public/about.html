About Us.

Analyzr was born out of a need to accurately and objectively summarize the mood of articles.
Being programmers, we read A LOT of tech articles, most talking about the same trending topics in the industry. And for a long time, we would compare these articles and their (sometimes justified) biases in one of two possible ways:

  • reading all of them, one by one, and developing a thorough understanding of each
  • delegating the reading of each article to friends, and then discuss them at length with eachother
However, we realized that both of these methods has a serious flaw: The reader's inner bias. The articles' true sentiment was lost in the distortion caused by our own biases, meaning that two people reading the same article could, and did, come to vastly different conclusions as to what the article truly felt about whatever was today's trending topic.
This problem wasnt only limited to the tech industry. Everything from blog posts to political articles are shaped not just by the writer, but by the reader as well. What we needed was a way to eliminate the leanings of the reader from the equation.
Analyzr does just that. Using NLP, we are able to gather information about topics the articles covered, and the articles sentiment about them, in a quick and objective manner. We then display the information in easy to read, yet informative, graphs and tables.
